    About Me

    I’m a PhD student at the Brain and Spine Institute (ICM), inside the Pitié-Salpêtrière Hospital in Paris. I’m working under the supervision of Fabrizio De Vico Fallani on brain connectivity which lays at the intersect between three main fields: neuroscience, signal processing and, of course, complex systems science.

    Brain connectivity aims at studing...

    Until now, I’ve been mainly working on building brain networks where both the nodes, that correspond to brain regions, and the edges, that link them, can be estimated in many different ways.

    Meaning going from brain electromagnetic signals or images to graphs. But since the brain is well known to expose various types of communication I would like go further in the analysis and apply the multilayer framework to better understand interactions between those types of communication.

    Now I understand better how all this works, and since the brain is well known to expose various types of communication between its components, I would like go further in the analysis of brain networks by using the multilayer networks framework. That’s why having Federico Battiston physically on my side would be a great opportunity for both of us.

    As preliminary results we were able to show that, in some cases, differences in populations could only be seen when considering all the subject’s brain networks at once in a multiplex topology. For that we built a multiplex for each subject where each layer corresponded to a frequency-band-specific brain network and compared groups using very simple indices describing the multiplexes topology.
